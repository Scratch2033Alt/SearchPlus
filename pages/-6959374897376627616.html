W3C publishes Verifiable Credentials 2.0 as a W3C Standard, making expression, exchange, and verification of digital credentials easier and more secure

Published: 15 May 2025

Verifiable Credentials is a family of specifications describing the extensible data model, as well as various mechanisms to secure Verifiable Credentials. The specifications cover a variety of security, privacy, and internationalization considerations for ecosystems that use those technologies.

Published: 26 November 2024

This press release announces our first grant since W3C's newly registered entity as a US public-interest non-profit organization. W3C is the recipient of a major grant from the Ford Foundation toward development of web accessibility standards, guidelines, and implementation resources to support access for people with disabilities.
